\section{Introduction}

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated unprecedented capabilities across diverse natural language processing tasks. However, the computational expense associated with serving state-of-the-art "frontier" models makes them economically prohibitive for high-volume applications. While smaller, local models offer substantially lower operational costs, they often lack the reasoning capability required for complex queries.

To address this challenge, we present \textit{Cascade Engine}, a generalized inference framework that implements multi-tier routing. Our approach shifts away from static, one-size-fits-all model deployments towards an adaptive system that triages requests across a continuum of cost-accuracy tiers.

\textbf{Contributions:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item We introduce a multi-tier routing architecture combining local, mid-tier, and premium LLMs.
    \item We present a suite of intelligent layers (Semantic Caching, Privacy Filtering, and Complexity Routing) to deterministically intercept and route queries.
    \item We provide a comprehensive evaluation against established baselines (e.g., RouteLLM) using the Alpaca-Eval dataset, highlighting significant cost reductions with minimal accuracy degradation.
\end{itemize}
