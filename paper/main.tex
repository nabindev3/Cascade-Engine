\begin{abstract}
Serving Large Language Models (LLMs) in production is often bottlenecked by the extreme costs of frontier models. However, not all requests require state-of-the-art capabilities. We introduce the Cascade Engine, an intelligent, multi-tier inference router that dynamically directs queries to the most appropriate model based on semantic complexity, privacy constraints, and cache hits. By integrating layers like DistilBERT for classification, Presidio for PII redaction, and FAISS for semantic caching, we demonstrate a Pareto-optimal trade-off between cost, latency, and accuracy against baselines like RouteLLM.
\end{abstract}
